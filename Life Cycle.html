Weevil's Life Cycle

The stages of their life include the egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages. Female weevils lay small, white eggs in clusters inside a grain kernel or on plants. The eggs hatch larvae, small worms, which feed on the grain. A larva grows inside the grain and becomes a pupa. It forms a cocoon and undergoes metamorphosis. Within a month, the weevil eats through the seed coat and emerges as an adult. Depending on environmental factors their life usuallys lasts for about a year.
